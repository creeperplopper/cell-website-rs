Plant Cells

What do they contain and how are they different to animal cells?

Plant cells and their content

Plant cells contain :

A cell membrane (mentioned in animal cells), a nucleus (mentioned in animal cells), a cytoplasm (mentioned in animal cells), mitochondria (mentioned in animal cells), chloroplasts which do the process of photosynthesis, a cellulose cell wall to give the cell strength and structure, a large sap vacuole which also help in structure and support but having other functions such as storage, waste disposal and growth; the tonoplast which is the membrane surrounding the vacuole.

However, not all plant cells have these parts (scientific name is organelle).
